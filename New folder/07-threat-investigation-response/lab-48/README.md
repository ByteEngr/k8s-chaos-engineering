## Lab 48 – Threat Investigation & Response: Final Incident Report and Handoff

### Scenario
You are closing a multi-day investigation. You must produce a **final incident report** suitable for technical teams and a **short executive summary**. Include timeline, impact, IOCs, containment, root cause (if known), and lessons learned.

### Investigation Objectives
- Summarise the incident in **chronological order** with sources cited.
- Separate **facts** from **hypotheses** clearly.
- List **impact** (systems, data, users) with confidence levels.
- Provide **remediation** and **preventive** actions with owners.

### Logs/Artifacts Description
You have access to:

- **Your prior notes** from the investigation track (synthetic bundle provided in class).

### Tasks
1. **Executive summary** (half page)
2. **Technical timeline** with key artefacts
3. **IOCs** (structured list)
4. **Containment and eradication** performed
5. **Lessons learned** (3 bullets) and **action register**

### Questions
- What belongs in the report vs an appendix?
- How do you write about uncertainty without undermining credibility?
- Who must approve release of IOCs externally?

### Indicators of Compromise
Deliver a **final IOC package** ready for detection deployment:

- Network, host, email, and identity indicators as applicable.

### Expected Analyst Outcome
By the end of this lab, a junior analyst should be able to:

- Produce a professional incident report and handoff package.
