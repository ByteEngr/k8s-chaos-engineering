## Lab 42 – Threat Analysis: Threat Briefing and Synthesis

### Scenario
Leadership asked for a **one-page threat briefing** on current criminal campaigns affecting your sector. You must synthesise multiple intel reports into **what matters for us**, **what to detect**, and **what to communicate** to staff.

### Investigation Objectives
- Extract **three relevant campaigns** with likely impact to your org.
- Translate technical IOC lists into **risk statements** for non-experts.
- Propose **detection coverage checks** (are we logging enough?).
- Define **what not to do** (panic-driven blocking).

### Logs/Artifacts Description
You have access to:

- **Two public or vendor intel summaries** (provided in class materials).
- **Your internal visibility map** (what logs exist).

### Tasks
1. **Select relevance**
   - Sector, geography, technology stack match.
2. **Prioritise mitigations**
   - MFA, patching, mail controls, EDR policies.
3. **Detection opportunities**
   - 5 detection ideas tied to available logs.
4. **Comms**
   - Short guidance for employees (3 bullets).
5. **Open questions**
   - What intel is missing that you would request.

### Questions
- How do you stay accurate when intel is incomplete?
- What is the difference between strategic and tactical recommendations here?
- How would you measure if the briefing changed behaviour?

### Indicators of Compromise
Include an **intel appendix**:

- IOCs worth monitoring internally (even if not blocking yet).

### Expected Analyst Outcome
By the end of this lab, a junior analyst should be able to:

- Produce concise threat briefings that drive decisions.
